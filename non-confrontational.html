Causes for your dog to be Non-Confrontational/ scared.


Lack of Early Socialization. One of the most common causes of a fear or phobia in dogs is a lack of early socialization. Negative Experiences. Negative experiences are another reason dogs develop fears and phobias. Genetic Factors. Genetics also plays a role in fearful behavior.

How to tell that your dog is Non-Confrontational?
  1. Tucked tail
  2. Ears back
  3. Licking the lips or nose
  4. Panting
  5. Low body posture
  6. Yawning
  7. Whale eye (wide eyes showing the whites of the eyes)
  8. Looking away
  9. Trembling or shaking
  10. Sniffing and grooming
