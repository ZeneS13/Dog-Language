Causes for your dog to be Stressed.


The 3 main causes are fear, seperation and ageing. Fear-related anxiety can be caused by loud noises, strange people or animals, visual stimuli like hats or umbrellas, new or strange environments,and specific situations. Dogs with separation anxiety are unable to find comfort when they are left alone or separated from their family members. Age-related anxiety affects older dogs and can be associated with cognitive dysfunction syndrome (CDS). In dogs with CDS, memory, learning, perception, and awareness start to decline, similar to the early stages of Alzheimerâ€™s disease in humans.

How to tell that your dog is Stressed?
  1. Pacing or shaking
  2. Whining or barking
  3. Yawning, drooling and licking
  4. Panting
  5. Changes in eyes and ears
  6. Changes in body posture.
  7. Shedding
  8. Avoidance or displacement behavior
  9. Hiding or Escape behavior
  10. Changes in bodily functions.

>

Exercise Your Dog. If your dog has separation anxiety, the obvious way to ease their mind is to never leave them alone. Physical Contact.
